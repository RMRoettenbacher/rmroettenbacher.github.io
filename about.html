Currently, I am a Yale Center for Astronomy and Astrophysics postdoctoral fellow at Yale University. I study stellar activity with a variety of techniques. From analyzing ground- and space-based photometry to directly imaging stellar surfaces with long-baseline optical interferometry, I aim to develop a detailed understanding of stellar activity and how it affects stars and their environments throughout their lives.

I was previously a postdoctoral fellow at Stockholm University, and I received my PhD from the University of Michigan.
